CCNP Training

(4.2/5)

The Cisco Certified Network Professional (CCNP) certification is an advanced-level credential offered by Cisco that validates a professional's ability to plan, implement, verify, and troubleshoot local and wide-area enterprise networks. This certification is ideal for network engineers, systems engineers, and IT professionals who want to demonstrate their expertise in networking concepts and operations. The CCNP certification is available in various tracks, including Enterprise, Security, Data Center, Collaboration, and Service Provider, allowing professionals to specialize in their area of interest within networking.

Course Description

The CCNP course provides comprehensive knowledge and skills required for networking professionals to design, configure, and troubleshoot enterprise networks. The course covers in-depth networking topics, such as advanced routing and switching, network security, automation, and network infrastructure. Depending on the chosen track, the curriculum may include specialized topics like network design, data center operations, or collaboration technologies. Through a combination of theoretical lessons and practical labs, the CCNP course helps learners master the complex networking technologies needed to manage today's sophisticated networks.

About exam & certification details:

What is the Process to Get CCNP Certified ?

Step 1. Choose a Track.
Step 2. Pass Exams.
Step 3. Study and Prepare.
Step 4. Schedule and Take the Exams.

What is the Exam Fee for CCNP?

Core exam: Around $400 USD.
Concentration exam: Around $300 USD. The total cost for the certification typically ranges from $700 to $800 USD, depending on the chosen path.

What is the Eligibility Criteria for CCNP Certification ?

A strong understanding of networking fundamentals.
Three to five years of experience in implementing enterprise networking solutions. Having a CCNA certification is helpful but not mandatory for pursuing CCNP.

How long is the CCNP Exam Duration ?

The duration of each CCNP certification exam typically ranges between 90 to 120 minutes, depending on the specific exam taken. The core and concentration exams each have their time limits and question sets.
